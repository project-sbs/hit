About Campus

HIT University consists of four main buildings: the Science and Engineering Building, the Grand Auditorium, the Arts Building, and the Humanities Building.

    • The Engineering Building at HIT University

      The Engineering Building at HIT University is a modern facility designed to foster innovation and collaboration. It features advanced labs, lecture halls, and collaborative spaces equipped with cutting-edge technology. Emphasizing sustainability, the building incorporates eco-friendly materials and energy-efficient systems. It offers hands-on learning in fields like robotics, computer science, civil, and mechanical engineering, and serves as a hub for academic excellence and research partnerships.

    • The Arts Building at HIT University

      The Arts Building at HIT University provides a creative space for students to explore and develop their artistic talents. With specialized studios for fine arts, music, and performing arts, it offers a dynamic environment for artistic expression. The building also hosts exhibitions, performances, and workshops, fostering a vibrant arts community within the university.

    • The Humanities Building at HIT University

      The Humanities Building at HIT University is dedicated to the study of languages, philosophy, history, and social sciences. Its modern classrooms and lecture halls provide an ideal environment for in-depth learning and critical thinking. The building encourages interdisciplinary dialogue, offering students a comprehensive education in the humanities while supporting research and cultural activities.

    • The Conference Hall at HIT University

      The Conference Hall at HIT University is a state-of-the-art venue designed to host a variety of events, including academic conferences, seminars, and large-scale meetings. Equipped with advanced audiovisual technology, it offers a comfortable and professional environment for speakers and attendees. Its spacious design fosters collaboration and knowledge exchange, making it a key center for intellectual engagement on campus.

Status of Membership in International Organizations

  • U21(Universitas21)

    Universitas 21 is a global network of research-focused universities established in 1997, with the aim of fostering research collaboration, sharing information, and promoting exchange among its member institutions. The network comprises 29 universities from 19 countries, including the National University of Singapore, the University of Nottingham, the University of Zurich, and the University of Sydney.

    http://www.universitas21.com

  • APRU(Association of Pacific Rim Universities)

    The Association of Pacific Rim Universities (APRU) is an international consortium of research-focused universities established to promote collaboration in education and research, while fostering economic, scientific, and cultural development across the Asia-Pacific region. The network comprises 60 member universities from 18 countries, including UCLA, USC, UBC, Peking University, and the Australian National University.

    http://www.apru.org

  • iCUBE(International Consortium of Universities for the study of Biodiversity & the Environment)

    A consortium of research-focused universities worldwide, established to advance research and education on biodiversity, climate change, and environmental issues.

    http://icubeconsortium.org

  • APAIE(Asia-Pacific Association for International Education)

    An international higher education association established in December 2004 to enhance exchange and cooperation among higher education stakeholders in the Asia-Pacific region and improve the quality of international education programs. Universities from 13 countries, including South Korea, China, Japan, the United States (Hawaii), Australia, New Zealand, Hong Kong, Taiwan, the Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Indonesia, and India, participated in its founding. HIT University actively participates in the annual APAIE Conference and Exhibition held every March.

    http://www.apaie.net

  • VIU(Venice International University)

    Venice International University (VIU) is a global consortium of 19 universities and institutions that share an autonomous campus located on the island of San Servolo in Venice, Italy. Based on an innovative platform, VIU fosters unrestricted global thinking and promotes international collaboration through diverse forms of education that transcend national boundaries.

    http://www.univiu.org

  • W100 Reputation Network(World 100 Reputation Network)

    The network consists of 46 member universities worldwide, focusing on building a reputation network and fostering partnerships through promotion, marketing, communications, and branding among the world's top 200 leading universities. It actively participates in annual conferences and other events.

    http://theworld100.com
